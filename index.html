Fall Detection System

Project Brief

A human factors solution for the prevalent issue of falls at the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia. This process required root cause analysis (RCA) through preliminary research into the common causes and locations of falls, quantitative data analysis of the hospital's data, and qualitative data collection in the form of patient and nurse surveys.

Over the course of one semester, my team provided three recommendations to reduce the prevalence of falls: (1) the redesign and relocation of existing posters to better inform families and nurses of the potential for falls, (2) a design improvement to existing non-slip socks that included Marvel or Disney characters to increase compliance, (3) a fall detection device that acts not only as an alert system, but also directly interfaces with the hospital system so that data regarding the metrics and location of the fall can be recorded. The prototyping process also included conducting a heuristic review of the device to ensure that the product would be user friendly for all parties involved, including nurses, family members, and patients.
